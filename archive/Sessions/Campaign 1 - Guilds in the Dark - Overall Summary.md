# Campaign 1: Guilds in the Dark — Overall Summary

**Party:** Paavu, Ryx, Iphitus, Yannick, Behelor and/or Lehinar (membership and roles inferred from the source material — not stated outright)
**Setting:** The Underdark, in an era predating Campaign 2 by an unknown span — this campaign appears to depict the founding/recruitment era of the Moa, whose surviving members (Behelor, Paavu, Ryx, Iphitus) recur as ancient, legendary figures by Campaign 2

*⚠️ Source material note: this summary draws on two sources of very different quality. The first is the player's raw notes ("Mythrir Campaign 1 - Guilds in the Dark.md") — a sparse, glossary-style list of names and one- or two-line fragments. The second is a set of direct excerpts from the campaign's Discord lore channel, including a DM-authored day-by-day timeline, which is far more authoritative and resolves several ambiguities the player notes left open (see "What the lore channel resolved," below). Where the two sources still disagree, both are kept on file and flagged rather than silently picked between.*

## A different kind of campaign

Guilds in the Dark is tonally distinct from every other documented era of Mythrir: a classic Underdark crawl, complete with illithids, an elder-brain-ascended No Name (Sitherak the Alhoun), bugbears, minotaurs, dragon turtles, flumphs, gelatinous cubes, and rival guild factions fighting over the dark beneath the surface world. Where Campaigns 2 through 4 lean tech-noir, this campaign reads as straight Underdark sword-and-sorcery — which makes sense given what it turns out to be telling: the origin story of the Moa, a name that recurs throughout later campaigns only as ancient legend.

## Into the dark — a timeline

The Discord lore channel preserves a rare day-numbered timeline for this campaign, anchoring otherwise-loose fragments to a real sequence. On Day 1, Paavu and Ryx meet a talking owl — affiliated, per the lore channel, with the Owls guild of "insane scribes of the supernatural." By Day 8 they're assembling an adventuring party to deal with trouble at Lake Urathil; by Day 12, Yannick is dispatched to scout the Owls separately. The full party meets at Gol'talum, a wild-west checkpoint city split between two rival families, on Day 45, then proceeds through Salamada (meeting Mackleroy) toward North Arill, fighting a neothelid en route and meeting "No Name" the Alhoun. By Day 48, an illithid infestation in North Arill is cleared with help from a human ally named Mort — who, unbeknownst to the party at the time, is the god of death Mor'Talus in disguise. Days 49–56 are spent on refugee relief and rest before the party reaches the Maw (called "the Maw of Urath" in the lore channel, "the Maw of Arrath" in the player's notes) on Day 58, where a False Hydra causes trouble until Day 71 — its defeat reveals statues of the Underdark's gods.

Day 72 brings Ruby the Glass Eye into the story: she charges the party with killing a Moa around the same time the original Silkworm turns up dead — explaining why later campaigns describe "Silkworm" as a title passed between successive guild leaders rather than one person's identity. The party heads west for desert guides (Day 73), meets the minotaur guide Mogus and company (Day 82), and defeats the Moa known as the Fallen Star — Corthus, or "Quorsys" per the lore channel's own uncertain spelling — on Day 84, with Paavu taking her Moa Coin and earning recognition as the newest Moa. At Gul'Malar (Day 86), Xanathar explains more of the Moa's history and charges the party with stopping "the Twins" (Typhlo and Urath) from destroying the world, gifting them a dragon called Xixxern so Ryx can settle some unstated old score — a strong candidate for the true origin of her later transformation into a dragon herself.

## Guilds of the Underdark

The lore channel also resolves a structural question the player notes only hinted at: the Underdark runs on ten named guilds, each themed after a bird — the Hawks (mercenaries, led by Vice Conroy), Condors (adventurers), Whippoorwills (clerics), Crows (thieves and spies, Silkworm's guild), Canaries (miners, led by Ruby the Glass Eye), Gulls (underground sailors), Parrots (pirates), Owls (scribes of the supernatural), Mockingbirds (bards), and the Moa — officially "a dead, ancient guild," though this campaign finds them very much alive, if scattered and secretive. See [The Guilds of the Underdark](../Locations/The Guilds of the Underdark).

## The gods beneath the gods

The campaign's deepest cosmology surfaces gradually: Typhlo and Urath are Twin Creator Gods of the Underdark, Avatars of Fire and Water respectively, whose mutual battle created the Underdark itself (the player notes frame this more starkly, as Urath being killed by Typhlo, who then buried himself in a volcano). The Watcher, patron of protectors and the unacknowledged power behind the Church of the Helm, reveals that life in the Underdark predates this battle and that he helped protect it from the fallout. Two more gods — Aerynath, the Patron of Life, a nine-headed dragon ancestor of Tiamat and Bahamut who often takes the form of a beggar, and Kythrazi, a two-headed bird-person patron of crafting who rose to godhood rather than being born — appear tied to ancient statues, with Aerynath manifesting as the beggar in the Maw to show the party an unbalanced set of scales depicting Typhlo and Urath. The implication is that the two creator gods are due to fight again, and that the resulting rift would let creatures "from the Beyond" pour into Mythrir.

Mor'Talus — a human-looking god of death flanked by two demons — is confirmed to be one and the same as Mort, the human ally who's been traveling with the party since North Arill, and consistent with the death god later known across Campaigns 2 onward as Mortalus. He notes that Kitar the Sphynx's time-warping ability is beyond even the gods. The party is given a set of artifacts nearly as old as the gods themselves, "because of our spirit," and is asked to choose among several world-changing Grand Magics the Moa can offer as a response to the coming crisis — Salazar's Mirrored Bastion, the Xanathar's Pocket Dimension, Kytaar's Sands of Time, the Nameless' Fungal Recruitment, Behelor's Breath of the Mountain, Skittarak's Killing Wind X, Polunaar's Move Reality, and an unnamed 12th-level "Blood of the Moa" — a choice the notes don't record the outcome of. See [The Grand Magics](../Items/The Grand Magics).

## Norathin, betrayal, and a god revealed

The party continues east, picks up Yannick near the Hall of the Owls (Day 96), and runs into an Avatar of Typhlo (Day 97) before reaching Norathin (Day 98), where they reunite with Mort and meet Behelor — confirmed a Tiefling — who sets them up to take out his rival, the Aasimar Moa Lehinar (Day 99). They defeat Lehinar and take his Fiery Crown (Day 103), then return to present proof to Behelor (Day 105) — only for an arrow to strike him the moment Ruby the Glass Eye arrives. Yannick is caught trying to sneak up on her and has his throat slit for it; Mort drops his disguise, revealed as Mor'Talus, and offers Yannick a second chance at life in exchange for taking over his pact. Revived and "uncaged," Yannick turns on Ruby, who reveals her true form — the Black Blood of Yag'saruf, "a terrifying ooze monster from beyond the world" claiming (per the player's notes) to be the real God of Death. Yannick defeats her and takes her glass eye, an act the lore channel says starts him "down a long path" — almost certainly the beginning of his rise from mortal thief to the lesser divinity later worshipped as One-Eyed Jack. Separately, Behelor is revealed to be "Baphomet," a Tiefling turned demon lord — a thread the notes raise without resolving against his earlier, more sympathetic framing as a Moa simply seeking Paavu's audience.

## Notes

*This summary draws on the player's sparse, glossary-style raw notes ("Mythrir Campaign 1 - Guilds in the Dark.md") and on more authoritative excerpts from the campaign's Discord lore channel, which resolved several open questions (Behelor's and Lehinar's species, "Mort"'s true identity, the guild structure, the Grand Magics list) but also introduced a few of its own unresolved discrepancies (the Maw's name, Lehinar's pronouns, several alternate spellings). Numerous threads remain genuinely open either way — the fate of the Typhlo/Urath rematch, the outcome of the world-changing spell choice, the full Moa roster. Several naming and identity conflicts surfaced while building this summary and the associated Characters/NPCs files — see the Mythrir — Character Index for the current flagged list.*
