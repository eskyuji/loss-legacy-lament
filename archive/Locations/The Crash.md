# The Crash

**Type:** City-state / desert region  
**Campaign Appearances:** CAMPAIGN 3 — Remnants of a Broken World

---

## Overview

A desert city-state ruled by Queen Nin, structured around a rigid class hierarchy: Rhox (large, sand-toned folk who never leave The Crash by tradition) at the top, ratfolk and vulture-folk in service roles, and a heavy population of catfolk refugees confined to the slums. A column of light burns day and night above the city, holding back the surrounding darkness and Rot.

The Crash and the Azure were historically one kingdom before the Empire split their funding and divided them; they have been enemies ever since, though The Crash's isolation (a side effect of the Rot/Darkness) has spared it from outlawing blood magic the way Varaneth and other regions did.

## Notable features

- **The Palace** — seat of Queen Nin, at the city's center, reached through the slums.
- **The Slums** — where the catfolk refugee population lives, a stark contrast to the wealth nearer the palace.
- **The Brown Oni** — an inn run by ratfolk and vulture-folk staff, named for Goroshi (the Brown Oni). Japanese-style Oni masks hang above each door — disconcertingly able to "watch" guests even through magical wards.
- **The desert beyond the walls** — home to sandworms, a swamp-buried Ziggurat/Necropolis (see below), and a stretch where Meeka's wandering Bazaar can sometimes be found.

## The Necropolis of King Nebbuzar

A floating, swamp-shrouded Ziggurat hidden beneath a thin layer of sand, home to the undead lich-king Nebbuzar — ruler of a pre-Empire desert kingdom destroyed roughly 5,000+ years before the present day. An anti-magic field surrounds his throne (a 30-foot radius in which non-artifact magic items stop functioning). Nebbuzar was betrayed by his own court wizard, Goroshi, who was later revealed to be bound to Nebbuzar as his phylactery. The necropolis collapses into the swamp once Goroshi is killed, freeing the undead dragon Tirgad, Nebbuzar's former king turned undead ruler of the horde.
