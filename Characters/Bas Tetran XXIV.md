# Bas Tetran XXIV

**Type:** Player Character
**Campaign Appearances:** CAMPAIGN 4 — Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn, CAMPAIGN 5 — Loss, Legacy, and Lament

## Character Overview

**Name:** Bas Tetran XXIV
**Class:** Swashbuckler Rogue
**Race:** Tabaxi (Snow Leopard)
**Campaign Setting:** Post-apocalyptic technological future

The twenty-fourth in a long line of Tabaxi bearing the same name. Operates like a modern-day spy — charming, suave, and professionally lethal. Takes jobs for whoever is paying but holds a quiet moral code and won't touch overtly evil work. Head of [House Tetran](../Factions/House Tetran), one of the oldest noble houses in Mythrir — the hereditary title "Bas" belongs to the house's ruling lord, making him both the origin and the namesake of an entire dynastic lineage. Later becomes Godblooded and immortal after touching Aleer's divine blood.

**House motto:** "The Moon Remembers."

## Weapons

### Yesiah — Main Weapon

+3 Artifact Rapier / Lazersword / Darksaber-style weapon. Tetran family heirloom. Katana-length, rapier-thin blade.

**Coiled Viper:** Braided leather band of snakeskin attaching another bladed hilt. +1 to atk and dmg. On hit, deals 1d6 acid damage and immediately attacks with the Coiled Viper at advantage, dealing 1d10 acid damage and preventing spellcasting for 1d8 turns.

*Visual:* Sleek tech-enhanced blade with a dormant black energy core running along the fuller. When activated, dark crackling energy bleeds from the channel — not glowing outward but consuming light, a black-violet edge. The Coiled Viper slots flush into the hilt base when dormant, its snakeskin cord wound tightly like katana wrappings. A single acid-green line traces the seam where it locks in. When activated, it coils out as a secondary attack blade.

### Rea — Offhand Weapon

+2 Silenced Pistol. Ignores reloading quality. Grants Extra Attack feature while paired with Yesiah. Critical hits with Yesiah also critically hit on the bonus action attack with Rea.

*Visual:* Extremely sleek, elegant silenced pistol. Matte black, minimal profile, long integrated suppressor. Custom, clearly expensive. No external markings.

## Campaign 4 Session Detail

Receives the sword Reno — worn down to almost nothing but forged of pure Tychonium — as a freely-given gift from Tyn, the Oathstone of Tenacity, in Lost Hope, just before the city goes dark around them. Strikes a deal with the Yuan-Ti near Ryxspine Lake, trading years of his own life for the dagger that becomes the Coiled Viper. Present for Aleer's death aboard the Heracles and the Tower of the Third Moon arc; touches Aleer's divine blood in the aftermath and is transformed into "the Godblooded" — immortal, marked with metallic-gold eyes and luminous blue lips, the first of his line to become more than mortal.

*⚠️ Naming note: this file's weapons section spells his blade "Yesiah," which closely echoes — or may be a deliberate homophone of — [Yeziah, the Sentient Platemail](../archive/Items/Yeziah, the Sentient Platemail), Tycho's sentient bismuth armor from Campaign 2, itself named for the Masculine Moon. Whether this is intentional dynastic naming or a spelling drift on one of the two items isn't confirmed in session — flagging rather than reconciling.*

## Divine Marks (Godblooded / Chosen of Ablation)

- **Eyes:** Piercing irises with a faint metallic-gold ethereal glow — subtle, like a reflection that shouldn't be there
- **Lips:** Cool, otherworldly blue — luminous and supernatural, as if lit softly from within. Not painted; divine.

**Divine rank (confirmed Campaign 5):** Demigod — specifically the **Champion of the Tetrans**, patron of his own sprawling bloodline (descendants reportedly numbering some 30% of the world's population by Campaign 5). Carries only a single drop of Aleer's divine blood rather than a full domain, which is why he sits below full godhood; what happens if he ever claims a domain of his own is an open question in-fiction.

**Origin of the drop (Campaign 5, Session 2, per 702 Purpose and Intent):** The drop was created when one of Feit's agents "punched a softball-sized hole through" Aleer — the act that both killed Aleer and made Bas into "Bas Tetran, the 24th, the God-Blooded." The agent responsible was never cursed with immortality and never got the chance to atone for it.

*⚠️ Naming note: 702's dialogue in the Session 2 transcript renders the name as "Elyr" rather than "Aleer." Given the two names' near-identical pronunciation and that this is an auto-transcribed session, "Elyr" is treated here as the same "Aleer" already established from Campaign 4 — flagging the variant rather than silently standardizing it.*

## Two Thousand Years Later — Campaign 5: Loss, Legacy, and Lament

### House Tetran and the First Advisor

When Bas returned after his 500 years of servitude to Meeka, he found that House Tetran had continued without him — his cousin had inherited the title as Bas Tetran XXV, and successive Lords and Ladies had ruled capably in the centuries since. He did not reclaim his title or contest the succession. He introduced himself simply as Bas, accepted the position of First Advisor, and has served every subsequent Lord or Lady Tetran in that role ever since. None have begun their reign without seeking his blessing — not because law demands it, but because none have wished to.

See [House Tetran](../Factions/House Tetran) for full house history.
