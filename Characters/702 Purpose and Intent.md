# 702 Purpose and Intent

**Type:** Major Character  
**Campaign Appearances:** CAMPAIGN 4 — Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn, CAMPAIGN 5 — Loss, Legacy, and Lament

*⚠️ Note: this file was originally labeled "Campaign 1," a leftover artifact from the first whole-compendium parse done before per-campaign chronology was established. "Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn" is Campaign 4's actual title — relabeled accordingly. There is no Campaign 1 material on file; the user has confirmed Campaign 1 notes have not yet been uploaded.*

---

## Overview

A Steel Wrought (Warforged) commander. Mahogany and granite body with red sparkling energy and white diamonds glowing inside. The de facto authority figure who draws the four protagonists together at the start of the campaign. Breaks the Warforged naming convention by having two nouns instead of adjective/noun — suggesting he is something more than standard. Later corrupted and used as a mouthpiece by Feit.

---

## Campaign 5 Session Detail

**Session 2 (first appearance in Campaign 5):** Originally head of security for Verenath during the earliest Warforged awakenings; later elevated to head of security for all of Mythrir under the Steelwrought banner during Campaign 4; now downgraded back to an ordinary Warforged and stationed in Verenath. Bas has interacted with him before. Encountered by the party as they leave the Cat's Pajamas for the goblin camp; immediately recognizes that Feit may be involved without being told, reasoning that "if the three of you are working together on something, he is certainly involved" — and volunteers unprompted that he has heard reports of an Owl and "a madman heading to [Shaidar's] rest." Warns the party to stay away from those who felt Feit's corruption before. Bas's Insight check (28) and Meeka both independently confirm he is withholding something, though not lying.

Reveals, unprompted and with startling emotion for a Warforged — let alone a former Steelwrought — that he still has recurring nightmares: *"For two thousand fucking years, I have had nightmares about what that bastard did to me and my kin."* Confesses that during the fall of Shaidar's Rest, under Feit's direct control, he broke the neck of a suckling infant — Feit's stated justification being that the city was "lost to the influence of" Za'ani, Bas Tetran XXIV, and Netiri, Guardian of Rot (see [The Emperor (Za'a-ni)](The Emperor (Za'a-ni)) and [Netiri](Netiri)). Notes that the infant's father had reportedly been "seen cavorting about with" [December Redd](December Redd) six years prior — the connection between that affair and the killing is left unexplained. Quotes his own final transmission from before Feit's corruption fully took hold: *"The future is here, the tower will claim us. Before me is death, and behind me non-existence. We knew what was to come and did nothing to stop it. Hubris is our downfall."* States that Verenath now watches for Owls from every rooftop, and that he would lead his own kind into the sea before letting Feit take them again.

Tells Bas directly that Bas carries "a single drop of divine blood" — given to him when one of Feit's agents "punched a softball-sized hole through Elyr, the God-Blooded," the act that made Bas into "Bas Tetran, the 24th, the God-Blooded." The one responsible was never cursed with immortality and never got the chance to atone; 702 and his kin have spent two thousand years trying to atone in his place. (See [Bas Tetran XXIV](Bas Tetran XXIV).)

Willingly steps into Magerna's Zone of Truth — cast around, not on, him — and intentionally fails his save to demonstrate good faith. Confirms he will personally file a false report, "I will look for a murderer that does not exist," to shield the mortal investigation from the truth, and asks in return that the party warn him if Feit has truly returned, "so that I can die rather than be controlled again." Thelonius, listening from behind the bar and visibly flipping coins in his metal hand — a combat-readiness tell, per Meeka — promises 702 will die by his own hand at the first sign of Feit-bondage returning; 702 accepts this without protest.

As he leaves, Magerna whispers a single unsettling line after him — *"How many times does someone get to have vengeance twice?"* — which visibly stiffens him before he continues out the door. Meeka separately reveals, without elaborating, that she holds an unspecified "piece" of him (his own word: "peace"), taken at some unspecified point, and has no intention of returning it: *"How he is now is what's needed."* Bas declines to use 702's face for the goblin-camp notification out of respect after hearing his story, borrowing a random lieutenant's face instead before abandoning even that plan once the scale of the massacre becomes clear. Later sent a span-read message informing him of the massacre; his reply is a detailed hand-drawn illustration of himself giving Bas the finger.

See [Campaign 5 - Loss, Legacy, and Lament - Overall Summary](../Sessions/Campaign 5 - Loss, Legacy, and Lament - Overall Summary) for full scene detail.
