## Overview

**CAMPAIGN 4 — Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn:**

A tall, pale Aasimar with completely black eyes save for metallic silver irises. Silver-blue celestial tattoos cover his skin. Long silver hair, shaved sides. Classically handsome and deeply charismatic. Carries an owl familiar named Nocturne. Was bound by an ancient Geass curse that forced him to serve Feit, bleeding from his tattoos of light as the price. Eventually freed and reformed, he becomes one of the most powerful members of the Frost Shepherds.

**CAMPAIGN 2 — The Eternal Empire:**

Same character as Campaign 4 but encountered here earlier in the timeline — before his corruption and binding. Introduced as an Aasimar sent by the Order of Iphitus. Born near Silver City to human farmers; worked as a meager EMT before the Crumbling, with his healing power growing dramatically afterward. His celestial nature is more visible and his powers less twisted. The arc of his corruption and eventual redemption spans both campaigns.

**Magic items / boons:** Carries the Sigil of Fo'el, his patron god, which manifests when he teleports. Makes a deal with a Yuan-Ti trader, trading away some of his "shine" (his light-based spells lose potency, his dark-aligned ones gain it) in exchange for +2 on concentration checks. Owl familiar Nocturne is briefly lost to necrotic damage near Myka's Keep before recovering.

## Campaign 4 Session Detail

A major antagonist arc this time: bound once again by a Geas, this iteration of Seraph is confronted by the party in Shaidar's Rest as the city falls to darkness. Meeka — God of All Lost Things — ultimately restores his soul, redeeming him a second time across his long, recurring cycle of corruption and rescue.

## Campaign 3 Session Detail

Reveals a past as a member of the Cloaked Hand, the Order of Iphitus's shadow-assassination arm targeting tyrants and evildoers. Casts Legend Lore to help The Magus research the Oathstones. Disguises himself as a vulture-folk to move through The Crash unnoticed, hiding his innate light behind a held torch. Mind-controls the Rakshasa Sebash after Sebash tries the same on Tycho, forcing him to reveal Goroshi's secret. Confirms that ordinary restoration magic cannot undo what Benji's spell does to Thelonius's missing arm — it's not damage, but a true erasure.
