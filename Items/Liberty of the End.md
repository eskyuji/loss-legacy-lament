# Liberty of the End

**Type:** Artifact — inactive, drawable via Divine Intervention
**Owner:** Formerly Seraph; currently on loan to Meeka (Campaign 5, one-week duration)
**Campaign Appearances:** CAMPAIGN 5 — Loss, Legacy, and Lament (referenced from earlier campaigns)

---

## Item Table

| Item | 5e Description | Visual Adaptation |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Liberty of the End | An hourglass artifact. Roughly eight hours of being left stationary fills its upper chamber. Known effects: an All Possible Futures effect (functionally similar to a Divination wizard's Portent), an Alternate Timeline effect (cast spells from a class outside the wielder's own, provided they're not shared), and Stars Without Number (once per long rest — once per week for Meeka's borrowed use — open a portal connecting two points on the current plane; arrive within 1d50 miles of the intended destination with a number of creatures equal to the caster's level). | Made of black glass. Each grain of sand sparkles as it falls — upward, not down. |

---

## Session 2 Session Detail

Originally Seraph's own artifact — its Stars Without Number effect was later folded into the mechanics of the Starblooded Hero prestige class more broadly. Now sits inactive in Meeka's domain along with (presumably) other former player-character artifacts, available to her once per week via her reworked Divine Intervention. Drawn for the first time in Session 2 and used immediately to relocate the party (plus a sleeping goblin child) from Veranath to 22 miles south of the Maw of Arrath.
