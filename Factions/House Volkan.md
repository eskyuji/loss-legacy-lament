# House Volkan

**Type:** Great House (master smiths — answers to no throne)
**Founded:** c. 1900 B.T.W. (~3,900 years ago)
**Founder:** Arcturus Volkan, First Forge Master
**Seat:** Hammerfalls, City of the Scarlet Peaks
**Sigil:** A black anvil beneath a seven-pointed forge star, framed by the crimson peaks of the Scarlet Mountains
**Colors:** Iron Black, Molten Bronze, and Crimson
**Motto:** *"What Is Forged Endures."*

## Overview

House Volkan is the oldest surviving Great House in Mythrir, founded in the aftermath of Madam Lanette's defeat nearly four thousand years ago. When Blood Magic was outlawed and the Scarlet Peaks abandoned as cursed, the Volkans climbed them willingly, transforming blood altars into foundries and temples into forges. Hammerfalls, their mountain city, has echoed with the sound of hammers ever since.

The Volkans do not merely forge weapons — they forge nearly every tool civilization depends on: swords, armor, plows, bells, bridges, anchors, crowns. A farmer's plow receives the same care as an emperor's blade. Their craft is measured by endurance, not glory. Every heir must forge a hammer before inheriting the House; if it cannot survive a thousand strikes, neither can they.

Despite supplying kingdoms across Mythrir, House Volkan answers to no throne. They hold an ancient privilege known as **the Right of Refusal** — kings, archmages, immortals, and even gods have been turned away from the Scarlet Peaks, always with the same answer: *"We do not forge what should not exist."*

---

## On Artifacts

No belief defines the House more than its understanding of Artifacts. The Volkans insist they have never forged one — they forge *masterworks*; history forges Artifacts. Every legendary relic was once an ordinary weapon on an ordinary anvil. Only sacrifice, consequence, triumph, and tragedy transform steel into legend. For this reason, House Volkan refuses all commissions requesting an Artifact outright: *"We can forge you a weapon. The Artifact is your responsibility."*

Within the Hall of Embers hangs a plain iron sword beneath a plaque reading only: *"Before history found it."* The House refuses to say which Artifact it became.

There is one secret known only to the Forge Masters: long ago, a Volkan attempted to forge an Artifact deliberately. The forge burned for forty-nine days. When the fires died, the smith was dead, the oldest forge split in two, and the weapon had vanished without a trace. The scar remains untouched to this day, a reminder that some things are never meant to be made by intent.

Every apprentice smith learns the same lesson: *"Anyone can forge steel. A Volkan forges purpose. Time decides the rest."*

## Connection to Campaign 5

Thelonius identifies the party's broken Tychonium shard as House Volkan work in Session 2, and sends the party to Milton's Feast hoping to earn enough favor to secure an audience with the Master of the Forges at the Scarlet Peaks — House Volkan's own seat, per this lore dump, confirming Hammerfalls as the destination Thelonius has in mind. Given the House's stated refusal to discuss which weapon became an Artifact, and its historical fear of deliberately-forged Artifacts, the shard's origin may prove a delicate subject if the party ever secures that audience.
