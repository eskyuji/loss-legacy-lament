# House Yufi

**Type:** Great House (patrons of architecture and art — no army, no throne)
**Founded:** 1,881 years ago
**Founder:** Lady Yufi, Master Artisan of the First Restoration
**Seat:** The Glass District
**Sigil:** A white crane standing beneath a blooming cherry tree, reflected upon still water
**Colors:** White, Vermilion, and Deep Jade
**Motto:** *"What Endures Must First Be Beautiful."*
**Campaign Appearances:** Background lore only — not yet encountered in session.

---

## Overview

Of all the Great Houses of Mythrir, none has done more to shape how the world *looks*. House Yufi has raised no armies and conquered no kingdoms — their victories are found in skylines: the soaring bridges of Empyr, the lacquered temples overlooking Lake Urathil, the stained glass cathedrals that catch the dawn, the marble gardens where nobles and beggars alike find peace beneath flowering trees. All of it began with a single line drawn on parchment by a Yufi.

The House traces its origin to Lady Yufi, an architect and sculptor of the First Restoration — the era when Mythrir rebuilt itself after the long decline of the Age of Technology. While neighboring rulers argued over walls and weapons, Lady Yufi insisted that broken people deserved beautiful places to live. She refused commissions from kings seeking monuments to themselves, offering instead to build libraries, bathhouses, public gardens, and theaters. Asked why, she is said to have replied: *"Children remember fountains far longer than fortresses."* The rulers who listened prospered; those who didn't governed cities that were safe but never loved.

---

## The House Today

House Yufi serves as Mythrir's foremost patrons of architecture, sculpture, music, painting, and design. No palace is complete without their consultation; no cathedral is consecrated before a Yufi has walked its halls; no monument earns respect unless it survives their criticism. To outsiders this reputation can read as arrogance. To House Yufi, it is simply responsibility — beauty shapes memory, and memory shapes civilization.

### The Living Gardens

Every estate maintained by House Yufi keeps a garden that is never truly finished. Each generation adds a single tree, removes a single stone, plants a single flower, repairs one broken wall. No heir may leave the estate unchanged — to inherit without improving is considered a failure of stewardship.

### The Artist's Oath

Every member of House Yufi takes the same oath upon adulthood — not before nobles or priests, but before a blank sheet of paper: *"May I never leave the world uglier than I found it."* The parchment is then burned; the oath is expected to live only within the one who spoke it.

### The Quiet Reputation

Among architects there is an old saying: *"Stone remembers the mason. Wood remembers the carpenter. People remember the Yufi."* Kingdoms rise and fall and the names of kings are forgotten, but children still laugh in gardens laid out by hands that turned to dust nearly two thousand years ago. House Yufi considers that immortality enough.
