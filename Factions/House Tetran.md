# House Tetran

**Type:** Noble House / Bloodline  
**Motto:** "The Moon Remembers."  
**Crest:** A snow leopard's profile set within a crescent moon, overlaid on a vertical spear/compass rose, framed by laurel branches and eight-pointed stars — all rendered in aged gold on deep navy. *(Crest image: save to this folder as `House Tetran - Crest.png`)*

---

## Overview

One of the oldest noble houses in Mythrir, House Tetran traces its lineage to a bloodline that predates the modern kingdoms. For generations, its heads bore the hereditary title **Bas** — a name synonymous with stewardship, diplomacy, and service to the catfolk peoples. The title passed down through succession as both name and office: each Lord or Lady Tetran became, in a very literal sense, the next Bas Tetran.

The most famous holder of that name was **Bas Tetran XXIV**, who disappeared into history during the final years of the old age while serving alongside the Frost Shepherds. As years became decades and decades centuries, he was declared dead. His cousin inherited the title as **Bas Tetran XXV**, and the House continued as tradition demanded.

---

## The Return

Five centuries later, XXIV returned.

He did not reclaim his title. He did not contest the succession. He simply introduced himself as Bas.

By then the name had ceased to belong to him. It belonged to the House. Recognizing that the family had survived and ruled — legitimately, capably — without him, he refused to disrupt centuries of governance. Instead he accepted the position of **First Advisor**, serving every successive Lord or Lady Bas not as their superior but as their counselor.

To this day, every Lord or Lady Tetran begins their reign by seeking Bas's blessing — not because law requires it, but because none have wished to rule without the counsel of the man whose name they bear.

---

## Notes on Bas's Role

The First Advisor position is, in practice, a formalization of the same instinct that defines his post-Campaign 4 life: staying involved, staying influential, and staying hidden behind someone else's name and authority. The House unwittingly gave him a legitimate institution for his coping mechanism — a way to be needed by the living while controlling the precise terms of that need. Every new Lord Tetran seeking his blessing is the living still requiring him, but at a distance he chose and on conditions he set.

That distinction matters for his arc. He already demonstrated, when he returned and stepped aside, that he is capable of letting go of something that once defined him. He gave up his name with apparent grace. What he has not released is the grief and guilt underneath — the burden Teyou's letter calls out directly. The structural release happened centuries ago. The internal one has not.

"The Moon Remembers" as a house motto is also worth holding against Bas's divine marks: his gold eyes and blue lips, both marks of Aleer's blood, both carrying lunar connotations throughout Mythrir's cosmology. His family name is built around memory as duty and inheritance. He has been living that motto for two thousand years whether he meant to or not. The arc of Campaign 5 is whether memory can become something he carries by choice rather than compulsion.
