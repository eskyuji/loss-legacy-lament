# Feit

**Type:** Major Character  
**Campaign Appearances:** CAMPAIGN 4 — Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn, CAMPAIGN 3 — Remnants of a Broken World

---

## Overview

Ancient, ageless, and deeply wrong. Appears as a figure in simple clothes with catlike eyes and elvish ears that point outward and downward. Light falls on him incorrectly — shadows where there should be light, light where there should be shadow. Has been spotted in famous paintings, historical photographs, and at every major event in Mythrir's history. An Owl who uniquely can remove his own mask. Exists in nine simultaneous versions of himself, each carrying animal tails on his belt. The embodiment of interference, inevitability, and stolen stories. Created by Yogg-Sothoth as order shed from chaos.

---

## Campaign 4 Session Detail

The major antagonist of Campaign 4. Revealed by Teyou Zhiang (the World Crafter) to be "the Dark Door" — order shed from Yogg-Sothoth itself, and the specific curse laid on Mythrir. Holds at least one version of Desiree Redd captive as a vessel in a temple in the Maw. The party spends much of the campaign recovering scattered pieces of an Owl Mask connected to him, including one piece surfacing in an embedded Cthulhu-mythos interlude (the Obali Chikro / Jane DeLaney / Necronomicon sequence), tying that seemingly separate plane back into the main plot. Teyou Zhiang identifies Feit as having usurped/co-opted Orthel (Teyou's shed madness) and names True Ice as a weapon capable of harming him. With Feit's defeat, Teyou Zhiang promises Mythrir will finally be allowed to reach the stars.

---

## Campaign 3 Session Detail

Appears to the party in The Crash, produces an owl mask so they might recognize him as one of the Owls, and delivers his usual brand of fatalistic philosophy about choice and inevitability ("It's as if your life is a mine cart pushed down the tracks"). Notably tells the party their camel (Goby) will enjoy the horseshoes among Tirgad's gifted loot — a small, oddly warm detail from an otherwise unsettling figure.
