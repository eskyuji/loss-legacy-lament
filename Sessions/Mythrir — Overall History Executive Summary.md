# Mythrir — Overall History: Executive Summary

A cross-campaign overview of Mythrir's documented history, spanning all four campaigns on file. Campaign 1's source material is notably sparser than the others — see the note at the end of this document.

---

## The shape of the world

Mythrir is a dying world being slowly unmade by the Nothingness, a creeping erasure of reality given form and will by an imprisoned entity called the Dark Bringer. Survival across the ages has depended on Oathstones — ancient artifacts or, just as often, people, bound by an oath that lets them anchor a region against the Nothingness's advance. Beneath all of it runs an older, stranger cosmology of "sheds" and "doors": gods and god-fragments (Yogg-Sothoth, the Dark Door, the World Crafter and its Aspects) whose politics and casualties ripple down through millennia of mortal history. Across every era documented so far, the player party has operated under the same banner: the Frost Shepherds, reconstituted again and again by different people in different centuries.

## Campaign 1 — Guilds in the Dark (earliest documented era)

The oldest period now on record is an Underdark guild war that, on its surface, looks nothing like the rest of Mythrir's history — illithids, rival thieves' guilds, fight clubs, and classic dungeon-crawl trappings instead of tech-noir. Its real significance only becomes clear in hindsight: it's the founding/recruitment era of the Moa, a name later campaigns treat purely as ancient legend. Paavu becomes "the newest Moa" after defeating Corthus the Fallen Star and taking her Moa Coin; Ryx, Iphitus, Behelor, and Yannick all appear here as well, decades or centuries before their Campaign 2 appearances as long-dead or legendary figures. The campaign also lays out the underlying cosmology behind several later naming puzzles: Typhlo and Urath, Twin Creator Gods whose mutual battle created the Underdark itself, and the title "Silkworm," whose original holder is killed here, explaining why Campaign 4 refers to a different person holding "the current" version of that name. A late campaign reveal ties two of the setting's biggest later figures directly to this era: Mortalus (the God of Death from Campaign 2 onward) travels disguised as a human ally named "Mort" for most of the campaign before revealing his true identity, and Yannick dies and is revived by Mortalus during that same confrontation — the likely origin point of his eventual rise to godhood as One-Eyed Jack. Source material for this era combines the player's sparse glossary-style notes with a more authoritative Discord-channel DM timeline, but several details (the Maw's name, a pronoun discrepancy on Lehinar, a couple of spelling variants) remain genuinely unresolved between the two.

## Campaign 2 — The Eternal Empire under the weight of the Nothingness and a five-locked seal holding back the Dark Bringer. The party of this era — Tycho, Myka, Enki Steelbane, Seraph, with Mortalus and Carp — recovers the five keys (tied to the gods Yorick, Nala, Juri, Un'Malo, and Orthel) across a string of escalating crises: the Hall of the Owls, the rebuilding of Varaneth, a Council collapse, and a strange detour that turns out to be the literal founding of Mythrir's Festival of Lights. Logan, a betrayer-turned-vampiric usurper, is destroyed at Myka's Keep, freeing Zeth Nogoth from a centuries-long curse. The era culminates in the party using all five keys to confront and kill the Dark Bringer directly — at the cost of Mortalus's life, Un'Malo's arm, and much of the world's remaining magic. The "Age of Gods" ends here; the third moon that appears in the sky during the final ritual becomes a permanent fixture of the setting going forward.

## Campaign 3 — Remnants of a Broken World (~1 year later)

A year on, the world is depowered but healing. Myka now rules Shaidar's Rest (the renamed Myka's Keep) alongside Tycho and Seraph, with Thelonius — master of all sixteen Metal Magics — as a recurring ally. A call for help from the desert city-state of The Crash draws the party into the orbit of the Magus, a sphinx trapped in unwanted undeath, who theorizes that Oathstones (not all of them yet identified) are the reason certain regions survive at all. The arc uncovers two: Goroshi, an Oni who turns out to be both a regional protector and a lich's phylactery, and Benji, founder of Shaidar's Rest, whose recovered body is found possessed by Logan's lingering spirit — destroyed for good this time. The campaign closes on the blood magic riot: a coordinated assault on Shaidar's Rest that costs Carp and Star their lives, costs Thelonius an arm permanently, and ends with blood magic outlawed across the region on penalty of death.

## Campaign 4 — Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn (~2,000 years later)

Two millennia on, Mythrir is barely recognizable. Magic has split into new schools, peoples have drifted into new forms (Steel Wrought, Rot Farmers), and Shaidar's Rest has flooded and fallen to a resurgent blood magic conflict, claimed by a new figure calling himself "the Pridemaster" who kills Zeth Nogoth on his way to power. A new Frost Shepherds — Bas Tetran XXIV, December Redd, Netiri, and the Emperor (Za'a-ni) — forms against this backdrop. The campaign's central reveal recontextualizes the entire setting's cosmology: Feit, an antagonist who has lurked at the edges of the world for ages, is "the Dark Door," shed order spun off from Yogg-Sothoth and the specific curse laid on Mythrir. Tycho and Myka, last seen alive in Campaigns 2 and 3, die again here and are remade as gods in Loanar's realm — the God of Death and Vengeance Incarnate, respectively — while Seraph cycles through corruption and redemption a second time. The campaign ends mid-confrontation with Geno Atul, the Ghost of the Third Moon, interrupted by an intervention from Fate itself — leaving the deepest layer of the setting's cosmology still unresolved.

## Campaign 5 — Loss, Legacy, and Lament (in progress, ~2,000 years after Campaign 4 / ~4,000 years after Campaign 2)

A new campaign is underway, picking up roughly two thousand years after Campaign 4's finale — per Tycho's own accounting in Session 1, that places it at roughly double the gap between Campaigns 2 and 4, or about four thousand years after the Eternal Empire. Bas Tetran XXIV, made immortal at the end of Campaign 4, has spent the intervening centuries first in 500 years of servitude to Meeka (the price of his rescue when the last Frost Shepherds were scattered fighting Feit), then in a long, ultimately fruitless search for his lost companions, and finally as a jaded puppet master running an underground network from the shadows. Session 1 opens on his standing once-a-century reunion with Tycho (now God of Death) and Magerna (now Aspect of Lament) at the Cat's Pajamas in Veranath, disrupted by the sudden, unceremonious death of Teyou Zhiang, the Worldcrafter himself — confirmed by Meeka as truly gone rather than merely lost. See the [Campaign 5 Overall Summary](Sessions/Campaign%205%20-%20Loss%2C%20Legacy%2C%20and%20Lament%20-%20Overall%20Summary.md) for full detail; this section will be expanded as the campaign continues.

## Throughlines across all four documented eras

A small set of entities recur across every campaign on file, often across thousands of years of in-world time: Myka, Tycho, and Seraph all appear in at least two eras and, in Campaign 4, are confirmed to persist as gods or god-adjacent figures well beyond their mortal lifespans. Thelonius spans Campaigns 2 through 4 as an Oathstone whose curse is, fittingly, Remembrance. Meeka, Loanar, and the Owls recur as agents of fate, loss, and inevitability regardless of era. The Oathstone concept itself — first theorized in Campaign 3 — turns out to extend much further back and forward than initially assumed, with Tyn, Tin, and Benji all confirmed or implied Oathstones by Campaign 4. And the Moa, founded or recruited in Campaign 1, persist as legendary, half-remembered figures from Campaign 2 onward — Paavu, Ryx, Iphitus, and Behelor are all "ancient" by the time the Eternal Empire encounters them.

## Notes

*This is a high-level synthesis only — see the individual Campaign 1, 2, 3, and 4 Overall Summary files for full per-campaign detail, and the Characters/, NPCs/, Locations/, and Items/ folders for entity-specific writeups. Several open lore conflicts (the Yesiah/Yeziah naming overlap, the Pridemaster title conflict, the Veranath/Varaneth naming overlap, the Aerynth/Aerynath naming overlap, the Behelor contradiction, and others) remain flagged rather than resolved — see the Mythrir — Character Index for the current list.*

*Campaign 1's raw notes ("Guilds in the Dark") arrived after this summary was first written, superseding an earlier version of this note which assumed no Campaign 1 material existed (an artifact of the unrelated "Campaign 1" mislabeling later corrected in Campaign 4's files — see the Character Index's structural note). A subsequent round of direct excerpts from the campaign's Discord lore channel — including a day-by-day DM timeline — further corrected this era's record, most notably resolving "Mort" as Mortalus in disguise (an error in this repo's first-draft reconstruction, since fixed) and confirming Behelor's and Lehinar's species. Treat the Campaign 1 summary as more solid than it once was, but still thinner than Campaigns 2 through 4's full session-prose sources.*
