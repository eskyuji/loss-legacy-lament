# Campaign 3: Remnants of a Broken World — Overall Summary

**Party:** Tycho, Myka, Seraph, Thelonius (recurring ally), joined partway through by Yelena Moller  
**Setting:** Roughly a year after the fall of the Dark Bringer and the Eternal Empire (Campaign 2) — the world is depowered, the gods are gone, and survivors are rebuilding across a shattered, slowly-healing Mythrir

---

## A year of rebuilding

In the year since the Dark Bringer's defeat, the world has begun to heal: Earth elementals work the edges of the Nothingness, slowly restoring land. Myka has settled into ruling Shaidar's Rest (the renamed Myka's Keep), now a flourishing, diverse city-state — the third most powerful in Mythrir — with four deliberately empty thrones in its hall. Tycho keeps the title of the Pridemaster; Seraph keeps the title of Lightbringer. Thelonius, still bound in his sphynx-cat curse, reveals he is the only living master of all sixteen Metal Magics (Alloymancy), including three newly discovered metals. Tycho's parents, Maginus and Bailey, are reunited at Verenath, with Bailey training under Thelonius as his apprentice.

## The Magus's summons

A Rhox infected with the Rot — Kwadwo — arrives from the distant desert city-state of The Crash, sent by a mysterious figure called "the Magus" to beg for help. Kwadwo snaps off his own horn for the party to deliver to his daughter, Kwa-mina, then asks to be sent into the Nothingness so he won't spread the infection further — a quiet, devastating act of sacrifice that sets the tone for the arc to come.

## The Crash

The party travels to The Crash, a desert city-state lit by a permanent column of light that holds back an encroaching Rot and Darkness. They find a rigid class hierarchy (Rhox at the top, ratfolk and vulture-folk in service roles, catfolk refugees in the slums) under the prickly rule of Queen Nin, who dismisses them until Myka forces her to kneel with a command-like ability tied to her crown. The real power behind the city's survival, though, is the Magus — once a Sphinx who sacrificed her life to save The Crash, only to be resurrected against her will by the Rot itself. She lives in permanent agony, fungus-ridden and effectively trapped in undeath, and wants only to find a way to finally die for good.

The Magus shares her life's work: a theory of Oathstones — ancient artifacts, or sometimes people, created by a one-armed crafter roughly a millennium ago, which she believes are what allowed eleven surviving regions of Mythrir to resist the Nothingness even though only ten Oathstones are known to exist. She suspects The Crash and the rival city-state of the Azure secretly share one. The party meets Goroshi, the ancient Oni who is the literal embodiment of The Crash's land and the inn ("The Brown Oni") is named for — and Seraph quietly notices that Goroshi himself may be an Oathstone, something neither the Queen nor the Magus has realized.

## Into the desert

Guided by Goroshi (who lives by strict self-imposed rules: he cannot kill, cannot lie, and cannot leave The Crash), the party treks north through the desert by camel-drawn dune sledge. They survive a brutal, near-fatal fight with sandworms — Tycho actually dies and stabilizes on a death save, earning a deep facial scar — and encounter Meeka, the wandering god of lost and forgotten things, in her bizarre desert marketplace. Meeka trades Tycho the Armor of the Oni for his cloak and gold, and unsettles Myka by threatening, almost playfully, to one day take her quiver, her bow, her name, and her friends. A silver rosebud nearby — tied to the goddess Loanar — tells Myka that "the Lost One seeks for you to join her," deepening the implication that Meeka and Myka are bound by the same cosmic wound.

## The Necropolis of Nebbuzar

The party discovers a half-buried, floating Ziggurat: the Necropolis of the ancient lich-king Nebbuzar, ruler of a pre-Empire desert kingdom destroyed some five thousand years earlier. Within an anti-magic field surrounding his throne, Nebbuzar reveals he engineered the coming darkness and rot himself, and refuses to be driven from his land. The party declines his offer to leave in peace. Soon after, a Rakshasa named Sebash — sent by Nebbuzar to stop them — is captured and mind-controlled into confessing the lich's greatest secret: Goroshi is Nebbuzar's phylactery.

Goroshi accepts this calmly. He has Yelena Moller — a new ally, a Tiefling bard summoned to his side as a "lost soul" — take up his Oath of Remembrance so the land won't simply lose its protector, then asks Tycho to execute him personally. The Necropolis collapses into the swamp the moment he dies, freeing Tirgad, an undead dragon and Nebbuzar's former king, betrayed and bound in undeath centuries earlier by his own court wizard — Goroshi. Tirgad agrees to a tentative alliance and gifts the party a substantial trove of magic items in exchange for retrieving bloodstones from the Scarlet Peaks, which the Magus needs to undo the Rot's grip on her body.

## The Oathstone of Eternity and the return of Logan

The party returns to Shaidar's Rest to track down a tenth Oathstone — the Oathstone of Eternity, which turns out not to be an item but a person: Benji, founder of the city, whose body and spirit separated upon his death centuries ago. With help from the cursed relic-hunters Sunny and Moony, they find Benji's spirit-medallion in the city's mausoleum — only to discover his recovered body is being possessed by the lingering spirit of Logan, his old apprentice and betrayer, previously destroyed by Myka's wish back in Campaign 2. Benji casts Time Stop to help the party finish Logan off for good, and this time he's sealed permanently in a binding jar — the same kind of vessel Logan once used to imprison Zeth Nogoth.

## The blood magic riot

While the party is in the catacombs, Shaidar's Rest erupts into chaos: hundreds of blood-magic users, many in foreign dress and including a striking number of red dragonborn, storm the city in a coordinated assault. Zeth Nogoth (Shidar) transforms fully into his shadow-monstrosity form and single-handedly cuts down attackers before the party even arrives. The cost is severe: Carp is killed outright, his revolvers left at his side; Tycho's squire Star is killed as well; and Thelonius is nearly killed, saved only by an irreversible spell from Benji that costs him an arm permanently — restoration magic simply can't undo what was done. Tycho's grief curdles into something darker: he bites a captured blood mage's throat and swears never to let this happen again.

In the aftermath, blood magic is formally outlawed across Verenath and other regions on penalty of death. Two new orders are founded to defend Mythrir going forward — the Shadow Mages, hunters trained in shadow magic, and the Steel Shots, metal-magic gunslingers. Tin, the Merchant King of Lost Hope, arrives to pledge solidarity and uses his own Oathpact to force the surviving prisoners to confess, then permanently neutralizes their blood magic with aluminum before releasing them. Most of the rest are executed by Zeth Nogoth and Tin together.

## Looking ahead

Benji clarifies that Logan's true goal was never to hold Shaidar's Rest — it was to destroy Benji himself, an Oathstone, as part of a long-simmering vendetta. With that thread resolved (for now), the party turns back toward unfinished business: returning to The Crash within their oath-bound deadline, retrieving bloodstones from the Scarlet Peaks for the Magus, and deciding how honest to be with Queen Nin about everything they've learned — Goroshi's death, the Magus's true nature, and the lich whose fall now leaves an undead dragon loose in the northern desert.
