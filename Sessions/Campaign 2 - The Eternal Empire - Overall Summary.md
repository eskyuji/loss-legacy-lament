# Campaign 2: The Eternal Empire — Overall Summary

**Party:** Tycho, Myka, Enki Steelbane, Seraph, with Mortalus and Carp joining for stretches  
**Setting:** Earlier in Mythrir's timeline than Campaign 1 — the Eternal Empire, before its fall

---

## The Empire and the Nothingness

The party — soldiers and agents of the Eternal Empire, organized in part under the banner of the Frost Shepherds — operates against the backdrop of a slow apocalypse: the Nothingness, a creeping erasure consuming the land mile by mile. The Empire itself is fracturing. Its Emperor, Za'anii, is bound to a cycle of ritual death and rebirth tied to a lunar eclipse, and with that rebirth approaching, the Empire's generals splinter into factions vying for power: some want to abandon the fight, some want to defend Malotia, others want to overthrow the Empire's command structure outright. Old gods and Aspects of the World Crafter — Un'Malo, Mortalus, and others — are openly active, and it becomes clear the Nothingness's master, a being called the Dark Bringer, is sealed away behind five locks tied to five gods: Yorick, Nala, Juri, Un'Malo, and Orthel.

## The Hall of the Owls and early threads

The party explores the Hall of the Owls, an ancient Moa ruin, fighting aberrant Beyond creatures and recovering significant loot (the statue gear later tied to Un'Malo, the Ring of Ryx, and more). Enki Steelbane gets his long-awaited reckoning with Hagorn the Red, the dragonborn who murdered his parents in the Ebonywoods. Taeus Merrymantle, Abacus Black, and others establish themselves as recurring allies and squadmates. Thelonius, presenting at this point as something closer to a courtly fixer than the figure he later becomes, anchors the party's ties to Varaneth and its Council.

## The Quiet Year and Varaneth

A long interlude follows in which the party helps rebuild and stabilize Varaneth — diplomacy, festivals, faction management, and the first appearance of Seraph, an Aasimar EMT-turned-healer who joins the Order of Iphitus and becomes a core party member. This period (documented separately as "The Quiet Year of Empyr") establishes much of the city's social fabric: the Council, the orphanage, Vincent's shop, and the warforged who live among the populace.

## The Council fractures, the wall, and the Nothingness made manifest

A tense Council meeting exposes the Empire's factional split in full: Maginus and several legions want to disengage, Aleer and others want to keep fighting, and a third faction under Alabaster the Cataster wants to depose the Empire's command and integrate with it. A Yuan-Ti survivor of an already-lost world and Behelor, last of the old Moa, both warn that retreat guarantees annihilation. Thelonius holds the remaining factions together into a pact.

On the Wall — the literal edge of the world, where land is visibly crumbling into nothing — the party comes face to face with the Nothingness itself: an unseeable, world-ending intelligence whose mere presence triggers mass panic and AoE damage, killing soldiers outright. It promises annihilation is inevitable. Diplomacy with a nearby orc encampment reveals the orcs are already in service to "the Dark Bringer," whom they call "the wall."

## The Festival of Lights digression

In one of the campaign's stranger turns, the party is pulled into a fully separate narrative — guided by the trickster/seer Akki Nakki — in which they personally become the origin of "the Festival of Lights," Mythrir's winter holiday. This involves a sleigh-riding "Red Man" who's been abducting fathers for a mountain coal-mine workforce, an incorruptible winter spirit named Jack Frost, and the deposing of "Saint Nic-clause," demigod son of Juri. The party installs Jack Frost as the new Saint in exchange for the children's freedom and an annual gift of joy — and learns afterward, from Akki Nakki, that the story was entirely true. They emerge with one of the five keys' lineage clarified: the keys belonged to Yorick, Nala, and Juri (recovered/accounted for), Un'Malo's key (held by Un'Malo himself), and Orthel's key — missing for millennia, last seen with a man in the north who lived to be sixty: likely Benji.

## The temple, the prophecies, and Friend

The party reaches a ruined city's untouched central temple, deciphers a string of cryptic prophecies (many of which reference party members directly — Un'Malo as "the fourth child," Tycho's bloodline, Myka's "glass eye," and others), and makes contact with a Beyond-native tentacled entity who calls itself Friend. Friend reveals it's an unwilling jailor of the Dark Bringer and that the seal binding the Dark Bringer is weakening. The party also learns of "the Dark Door," a Beyond god of transit whose escape would open Mythrir permanently to Beyond incursion.

## Myka's Keep and Zeth Nogoth

The party is drawn to Myka's Keep — abandoned for centuries, watched over by the self-blinded centaur Rynn — and fights through a corrupted, shadow-infested approach guarded by blood-magic wards. At its heart, they confront Logan, a vampiric usurper who claims the Keep by "Rite of Conquest" and who once cursed a loyal protector, Zeth Nogoth (then known as Nogash), into a shadow-bound prison for centuries. Myka — armed with a final letter from the long-dead Benji that anticipates this exact moment — wishes Logan into disintegration. Zeth Nogoth is freed, declines (for now) to face Rynn out of shame, and stays behind to hold the Keep against an incoming wave of Beyond creatures as the party retreats.

## The Council, the fifth key, and Un'Malo's fall

Back in Varaneth, the Empire's surviving Council factions formally pact together as the Frost Shepherds, with the Emperor Za'anii granting the party a fiefdom and inviting them to select a fifth member to complete a five-key ritual. The party chooses Mortalus, who grants the **Boon of Mortalus**. Immediately afterward, in a brutal ambush, "the defenders of this place" — implied agents of the Dark Bringer — slaughter Council guards and assassinate Un'Malo, severing his arm with a Benji-touched blade. Mortalus drops his composed illusion, revealing his true skeletal death-god form, and the party flees toward the temple as the Emperor's ritual eclipse goes wrong: a third moon appears in the sky.

## The Dark Bringer

The party crosses into the temple — now grown eyes and a fleshy maw, with the prophecies on its walls shifting to foretell their failure — and uses all five keys (Tycho: Yorick's, Mortalus: Un'Malo's, Enki: Nala's, Myka: Orthel's, Seraph: Juri's) to open the seal. They are drawn into a featureless Nothing and confront the Dark Bringer directly. Mid-fight, Mortalus sacrifices himself to fully restore the party, and Tycho — deemed unworthy of the Greatsword of the Pridemaster while mind-controlled — takes up Mortalus's fallen scythe instead. The Dark Bringer is destroyed.

## Aftermath

The party returns to find Varaneth had been fighting nonstop for three weeks in their absence; several heroes (Aerynth, Kithrazi, the Watcher) have fallen, the Emperor was never reborn, and the third moon remains fixed in the sky — "the Age of Gods is over." More land — including Varaneth, Myka's homestead, and a town in Yorastova — has been lost to the Nothingness. Admiral Taeus Merrymantle, now ranking officer by attrition, uses the heart of an Onyx dragon (dropped by the Dark Bringer) to begin a roughly year-long ritual restoring Mythrir's broken land. Zeth Nogoth, alone at Myka's Keep, reports he's fused shadow magic with nature to make the land itself defend the Keep — though Myka's territory continues shrinking. The world is left depowered: the Yuan-Ti and their magic are gone, the old gods are gone, and the party closes the campaign uncertain where to go from here.
