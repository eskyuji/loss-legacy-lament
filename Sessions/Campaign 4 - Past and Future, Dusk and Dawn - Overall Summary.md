# Campaign 4: Past and Future, Dusk and Dawn — Overall Summary

**Party:** Bas Tetran XXIV, December Redd, Netiri, The Emperor (Za'a-ni)
**Setting:** Roughly 2,000 years after the Eternal Empire / the fall of the Dark Bringer (Campaigns 2–3) — races and cultures have drifted (Steel Wrought, Rot Farmers), magic has split into six schools (Allomancy, Shaidomancy, Hemomancy, Putremancy, NullMancy, and the now largely-extinct Philomancy of Oaths), and the old gods, Oathstones, and Owls resurface in new, stranger forms

---

## A changed world

Two thousand years on, Mythrir is unrecognizable in places and eerily familiar in others. Shaidar's Rest has flooded, and blood magic — outlawed since the riot in Campaign 3 — has resurged amid a Tiefling-vs-Leonin caste conflict. A new figure calling himself "the Pridemaster" rises to power there and kills Zeth Nogoth, protector of shadows for centuries, on his way to claiming the city. The Tower of the Owls, dormant for ages, reappears. Bas Tetran XXIV (the latest in a long line of Tabaxi bearing the name), December Redd (a shifter haunted by his missing daughter Desiree), Netiri (a Satyr druid of the Rot Farms), and the Emperor (Za'a-ni, a scrawny armless Leonin artificer running a scrapyard) are drawn together as the new Frost Shepherds.

## Dreams, omens, and Lost Hope

A bar scene and shared dream sequence — featuring an earlier Bas Tetran, The Magus, Meeka, and visions of the "Halls of Yeziah" and an old Emperor of Mythrir — sets an unsettling tone before the party even leaves. Their road takes them through Lost Hope, where an altar on Tyn Street honors Tyn, the Oathstone of Tenacity, present since the city was a fifty-foot patch of dirt. Tyn gives his sword Reno — pure Tychonium, worn to almost nothing — to Bas freely, just before the city goes dark around him. Nearby, the party encounters One-Eyed Jack and a monstrous asphalt-and-darkness creature, and meets Tyn properly for the first time.

## Owls, the Yuan-Ti, and first encounters with Feit

The party's path repeatedly crosses the Owls — agents of fate and inevitability — and Feit himself, ancient and ageless, revealed by campaign's end to be "the Dark Door," order shed from Yogg-Sothoth and Mythrir's specific curse. Near Ryxspine Lake, the Yuan-Ti who hunts Feit across time strikes a deal with Bas, trading years of his life for the dagger that becomes the Coiled Viper.

## Death, deification, and the Truthspeakers

In a major interlude in Loanar's realm, two recurring figures from earlier campaigns — Tycho and Myka — die and are remade: Tycho confirmed as the God of Death, Myka as Vengeance Incarnate. Galv, a Truthspeaker working under Nakki, becomes a crucial ally as Shaidar's Rest falls fully to darkness, fueling his greatest teleportation with the imprisoned soul of Madam Lennette, on loan from Meeka.

## The fall of Shaidar's Rest and Seraph's reckoning

Shaidar's Rest succumbs to darkness outright. Seraph — corrupted once again, bound by a fresh Geas — becomes a major antagonist before the party confronts him directly; Meeka ultimately restores his soul, redeeming him a second time. Downtime afterward includes an Aesir-naming sequence and the introduction of Varyn's reformed Moa party, who arrive bearing the severed head of one of Feit's nine simultaneous selves as a greeting gift — courtesy of Tin, who charged Varyn with refounding the order.

## Meeka's bazaar, the Maw, and Veranath

At Meeka's infinite bazaar, Madam Lennette's doll changes hands and four Moa pendants tied to Loanar's lore are distributed to the party. The trail to find Desiree Redd leads to the Maw, a region gone dark and home to a False Hydra — she's found alive, imprisoned in a temple as a vessel for a version of Feit, revealed along the way to be a Truthspeaker with her father's fire. Elsewhere, the party reaches Veranath — a dead, abandoned city now ruled by a cat hive-mind under Thelonius, the Oathstone of Remembrance — and crosses the Gates of the Afterlife into the death-world, meeting Enki Steelbane, Un'Malo, and the All-Seeing. An embedded, seemingly separate interlude (Obali Chikro, Jane DeLaney, James Stanford/Henry, Necronomicon and Azathoth references) turns out not to be separate at all — a recovered piece of an Owl Mask ties it directly back to the main plot.

## The Heracles, the Godblooded, and the World Crafter's truth

Aboard Aleer's airship, the Heracles, the party travels to the Tower of the Third Moon. Aleer dies there at the hands of a corrupted Seraph; Bas and Netiri touch his divine blood in the aftermath, and Bas becomes "the Godblooded" — immortal, marked with metallic-gold eyes and luminous blue lips. Teyou Zhiang, the half-orc World Crafter, delivers the campaign's central cosmological reveal: Feit is the Dark Door, shed order of Yogg-Sothoth; Orthel was Teyou's own shed madness, since usurped by Feit; and True Ice — the substance in Teyou's jar — is one of the only weapons that can hurt him.

## Empyr and the note from Fate

Smuggled into the corrupt city of Empyr by Silkworm, leader of the Thieves' Guild, the party passes a rot farm tended by the Greatshell Myconid Rotguardian Ixtar before reaching the ruins of Zaani's (the Emperor's) old throne room. There, Geno Atul — the Ghost of the Third Moon, a leonin skeleton seated on the throne with a bismuth dagger through its chest — claims to be the living consequence of Zaani's sins. The fight is interrupted mid-battle by a swarm of paper owls bearing a note from Fate, who refuses to let the party lose another piece from the board — leaving the confrontation, and much of the campaign's deepest cosmology, unresolved as play continues.
