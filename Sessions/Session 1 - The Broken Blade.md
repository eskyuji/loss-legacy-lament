# Session 1 — The Broken Blade

*A goblin possessed by a shattered Tychonium shard attacks a beggar trapped in a time loop — and Feit's name resurfaces for the first time in two thousand years.*

Part of [Campaign 5: Loss, Legacy, and Lament](Campaign%205%20-%20Loss,%20Legacy,%20and%20Lament%20-%20Overall%20Summary). See all sessions at [Session Recaps](Recaps).

---

### Going through a god's pockets

With the space-between-moments over and Tycho and Thelonius both stepped back into their own duties, Bas searches Teyou's body properly for the first time. He finds only a small coin pouch — 37 electrum pieces, a currency nobody's used in millennia (Teyou had tried to pay for the round of drinks with it; the bar gave it to him on the house) — and a three-inch sliver of Tychonium, silver-gray metal that transmutes ordinary iron wherever Tycho's influence runs strong, tougher and more receptive to crafting than the iron it replaces. In the course of working out what it is, several older threads get tied off: Tychonium, Seraphium, and Myconium are confirmed as the three metals of "the Volkans," a master-smithing tradition whose work is stamped and famously unbreakable ("their swords don't break"); Seraphium and Myconium have both become vanishingly rare because the entities whose influence produced them are both currently lost; and Reno — the worn Tychonium sword Tyn gave Bas back in Campaign 4 — is confirmed to have eventually been melted down and reforged into a bismuth breastplate for Emp, the Emperor (Za'a-ni).

The same conversation surfaces a harder fact: Seraph's status is confirmed as **lost**, in the same unresolved category as Netiri and Za'ani — last known present at the Frost Shepherds' final confrontation with Feit before being scattered along with everyone else. No evidence of death; no evidence of survival either.

Magerna, for his part, goes to Thelonius first with the practical question of where to put a dead god's body. ("Would you like to keep a god of all things in your basement, or where do you think we should bury him?") Thelonius, deadpan, offers the freezer, then mentions in passing that "the gates" open "about three months before the march" — a specific-sounding timeframe neither Bas nor Magerna follows up on in the moment. Meeka ultimately takes the body herself, folding it into her own mass and sinking through the floorboards to her domain — it doesn't belong in this world at all, in her words.

### A city repeating itself

Bas's insight check on the beggar the previous session — a 28 — hadn't been enough to get a read on him, and that unfinished business is enough to send Bas after him on foot, with Magerna tagging along ("I did not sense that justice or vengeance is required of this man, but I too have questions"). Tailing him at a distance through the early evening streets, they overhear a vendor-and-customer exchange over the price of copper — and then, turning a different corner entirely, hear a different vendor and a different customer have the *exact same argument*, word for word. It happens a third time.

A history check from Magerna and Meeka (rolled involuntarily — "the words spill out of their mouths without them realizing it") surfaces the term for what they're seeing: **Synchronization Malformata** — the phenomenon in which reality begins to repeat, overlap, or contradict itself; strangers speaking identical conversations, places feeling inexplicably familiar, overwhelming and impossible déjà vu. Scholars who study it don't consider it illusion — it's reality "briefly remembering another version of itself." The more frequent and severe the malformations, the closer the world sits to what the phenomenon's own scholars call a catastrophic convergence. Meeka can think of only one recent event significant enough to have triggered it: the Worldcrafter's death, that same night.

### The owl on the rooftop

Perception turns up a robed figure crouched on a rooftop two blocks ahead — a pewter owl mask catching the light of the twin moons, frantically filling scroll after scroll, each one folding itself into an origami owl and flying north toward the Hall of the Owls the instant it's done. No one has seen an owl in two thousand years — not since the Owls vanished at the exact same moment Feit did, during the final battle in which the Frost Shepherds and Seraph were lost inside the Hall of the Owls itself. This one is, per Magerna, plausibly one of the originals, returned to the Owls' purpose before Feit's corruption: watching, and recording.

It is also completely untouchable. Moonbeam connects and does nothing but singe its robe. A called-shot rapier strike lands true and does nothing. A point-blank shot from Rea, fired mid-backflip with perfect aim, simply misses — "you were shooting where he is, not where he will be." Meeka's Banishment forces it out of existence for a fraction of a second before it rises back out of its own dropped scroll case, still writing. It never reacts to any of it — it just chronicles.

### A blade that shouldn't exist

While the party is distracted by the owl, a goblin — later revealed to be no older than sixteen or seventeen — rushes the beggar out of nowhere and stabs him with a crude shiv. Magerna reaches him in time to lay Death Ward and, a moment later, the Empathy ability (redirecting any damage the beggar takes onto himself) plus Sanctuary. It's not enough to stop the attack: every time the makeshift blade glows, the goblin's own wounds knit shut and he keeps stabbing, ignoring a Command spell outright when the glow overrides it. The blade cuts through armor and temporary hit points alike, for exactly one point of damage each time — old-feeling wounds on new hits, like an ache before rain.

Bas ultimately ends it by severing the goblin's bladed hand outright with Yesiah. The goblin withers to dust on the spot — confirming, grimly, that whatever it was by that point, it wasn't really him anymore — and a thread of black smoke pours out of the wound and back into the blade. What's left is a broken shard, six inches long, curved, stamped with a Volcan maker's mark and a single Volcan rune: **Remember**. Investigating it directly, Bas alone hears a voice say, clearly: *"You are nothing without me."* No one else hears it. Held near the Tychonium sliver taken from Teyou's own pocket, the two pieces visibly repel each other, like magnets of the same pole.

Magerna heals the beggar fully with Cure Wounds, but his mind is broken beyond a cleric's reach — Break Enchantment finds nothing to break; he was never charmed or possessed. What comes out instead, once he's lucid enough to speak, is that he's been living the same day on a loop: wake up, beg, watch Teyou drink at the same table, die every night, start over. Today is the first time Teyou has ever actually died. Something changed. He gets up, says he's going to the Hall of the Owls, and walks north into the dark. Magerna's parting words to him: *"I will remember you and I will mourn you."*

Wary of touching the shard again, Meeka wraps it in her own refuse and sends it back to her domain rather than let it stay on the street. When she returns, it's in an entirely new, deliberately constructed body — no longer a drifting mass of trash with a floating face, but a stitched, patchwork humanoid form: skin pieced together from snakeskin and the discolored hide of many different creatures, with black void visible in the gaps between the stitching. She confirms what the shard's voice already implied: *"He's back."* Feit — sealed away, by Bas's own accounting, in that final confrontation two thousand years ago — is the only one who could have said those words.

The session ends with the three of them heading back to the Cat's Pajamas to bring Thelonius up to speed.
